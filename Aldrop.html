ALDROPS

Aldrops, also known as door bolts or door latches, are essential hardware components used to secure doors. They typically consist of a metal bar or rod that can be moved into position to lock or unlock a door. Aldrops are commonly used in homes, offices, and various other buildings to provide an additional level of security.


The primary function of an aldrops is to hold a door firmly in place when it is closed. They consist of a metal rod or bar that extends from one side of the door to the other and fits into a strike plate or catch on the door frame. When the door is shut, the aldrops is engaged by sliding the rod or bar into the strike plate, effectively securing the door in a closed position.


Aldrops come in various designs and sizes to accommodate different door types and styles. They can be made from different materials such as brass, stainless steel, or iron, offering durability and strength. Some aldrops may also feature decorative elements, adding aesthetic appeal to the door.


Overall, aldrops play a crucial role in enhancing the safety and privacy of doors. They are a practical and reliable choice for securing various types of doors, be it entrance doors, interior doors, or gates.
