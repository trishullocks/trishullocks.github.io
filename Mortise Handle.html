MORTISE HANDLES

A Mortise handle is a type of door handle that is commonly used in residential and commercial buildings. It is named after the mortise lock, which is the type of lock that it is designed to work with. A mortise handle is installed on a door that has been prepared with a mortise pocket or recess, where the lock mechanism is housed.


The mortise handle typically consists of a lever or knob for operating the door, along with a backplate that covers the mortise pocket. The backplate is often decorative and can be made from various materials such as metal, wood, or plastic. The lever or knob is attached to a spindle that connects to the mortise lock, allowing the handle to engage and disengage the lock mechanism.


One of the key advantages of a mortise handle is its durability and security. The mortise lock, being housed within the door, provides a stronger and more secure locking mechanism compared to surface-mounted locks. The handle itself is typically sturdy and designed to withstand frequent use and wear.


Mortise handles come in a wide range of styles, finishes, and designs, allowing for customization to match the aesthetics of the door and the surrounding decor. They are available in various shapes, sizes, and configurations to suit different door types and personal preferences.


In summary, a mortise handle is a type of door handle that is specifically designed to work with a mortise lock. It provides durability, security, and aesthetic appeal, making it a popular choice for both residential and commercial doors.
